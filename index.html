In the realm of music, where sounds are as varied as the colors of a rainbow, there exists an enigmatic entity known as Therum. This is the humble yet intriguing endeavor of John Luke Melovich, a multi-instrumentalist who prefers to let his music speak for itself. Melovich is not just a musician; he's a sonic explorer, traversing the vast landscape of sound with an open mind and adventurous spirit.
Therum first surfaced with "Two Sunken Sons," a debut album that emerged from Melovich's high school years. This early work, while showcasing Melovich's raw talent and dedication, is a far cry from the Therum of today. Like a river that changes course over time, Therum's sound has evolved, becoming more complex and unpredictable.
Hints of Radiohead's introspection, Tame Impala's psychedelia, Muse's grandeur, and The Strokes' raw edge can be discerned in Therum's music, but these are mere echoes. Melovich doesn't mimic; he absorbs, distills, and reinvents. The result is a unique fusion, a sound that defies categorization and challenges the boundaries of indie rock.
Now a university student, Melovich continues to evolve Therum, his creativity an ever-flowing stream. Each new track is a step forward, a testament to his commitment to exploration and growth.
